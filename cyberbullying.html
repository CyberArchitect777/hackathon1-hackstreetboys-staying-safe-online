Understanding Cyberbullying & Harassment

Cyberbullying is a form of bullying that takes place online. It can include mean text messages, hurtful social media posts, or even spreading rumors through email. Harassment, on the other hand, is a more serious and persistent form of bullying that can have legal consequences.

Both cyberbullying and harassment can have a significant impact on a person's mental health. It's important to understand the signs of cyberbullying and harassment, and to take steps to protect yourself online.

Signs of Cyberbullying

  • Decrease in Device Usage
    -     One of the biggest indicators of cyberbullying is an unexpected hesitation to use their devices. If a person suddenly seems disinterested in a device, it's a sign that they're being attacked.
  • Sudden Withdrawal from Social Media
    -     Sometimes, bullied individuals withdraw from social media entirely. They may suddenly delete Facebook or Instagram profiles. Parents should be aware of all of their children's accounts.
  • Reluctance for Activities
    -     A person might be reluctant to leave the safety of their home. Also, they may avoid interactions with their peers. Or they may no longer show interest in activities they usually enjoy.
  • Emotional Changes
    -     Frustration and sadness can often indicate an online attack. That's especially true if it's directed towards devices. Often, bullied individuals show signs of depression and anxiety. Other emotional changes include sudden anger and lashing out.
  • Unwillingness to Communicate
    -     An individual might be jumpy and unwilling to speak to a friend, relative, partner or colleague. This may be especially true when it comes to talking about social media. Often, they fear how they will be viewed. Also, they might not want to create a more complicated situation, especially with work & relationships.
